Grab Community

Custom Typeface as a Brand-Building Tool

“Grab Community: Custom Typeface as a Brand-Building Tool” from BITS10 is now on YouTube! In this group discussion panel, our type director Sovichet Tep, the designer responsible for Grab Community Khmer; Carina Teo, the Head of Creative at Grab; Tan Sueh Li of hrftype, the designer responsible for Grab Community Latin and Vietnamese, and moderator Anuthin Wongsunkakon of Cadson Demak, came together to have conversations about the thought process behind Grab's rebranding, the creation of the Grab Community typeface, and the process of localizing them into many other Southeast Asian scripts such as Thai, Khmer, and Burmese

Granshan Competition

Script Specialists: Southeast Asian Scripts

Our Type Director Sovichet is Part of the Granshan Competition’s Judge Panel We are beyond excited to announce that our type director Sovichet Tep will be one of 8 Southeast Asian Script Specialists for the Granshan Competition, curating the world’s most exceptional typefaces beyond Latin; alongside Sasikarn Vongin, Suppakit Chalermlarp, Sirin Gunkloy, Tristan Hinkel, Sasikarn Vongin, Jinda Nuangjumnong, Fiona Ross, and Panuwat Usakulwattana. Submit your next great type before 29 February!

The panel brings deep expertise in regional script design and typographic innovation.

Brand Identity

Designing A Brand Identity That Actually Slaps

Designing A Brand Identity That Actually Slaps: Slaphouse Slaphouse is a dynamic and emerging record label in Cambodia, featuring talented artists like Kwan, Maifern, Vito, M-Fatt, Mann Doss, and others. By pushing the boundaries of the Khmer sound, Slaphouse artists have achieved great success, amassing millions of views. We're thrilled to have worked with Slaphouse on the design of their brand identity, capturing their distinct, high-energy, and unapologetic spirit. Additionally, we worked with Sokai Yoon, a Khmer-American designer from Atlanta on the photography and art direction of Slaphouse's artist photoshoot.

From color systems to typographic rhythm, the identity is built to be bold, playful, and memorable.
